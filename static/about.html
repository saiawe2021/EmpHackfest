Become an Astronaut

Your Journey to the Stars Starts Here

Requirements

To become a NASA astronaut, you must meet the following basic requirements:

  • U.S. citizenship
  • A master's degree in a STEM field
  • At least two years of related professional experience, or 1,000 hours of pilot-in-command time in jet aircraft
  • The ability to pass NASA’s long-duration spaceflight physical

General Information

NASA's astronaut candidates come from a variety of diverse backgrounds, including engineering, medicine, and the military. Training includes intense physical and mental preparation, simulation missions, and learning to operate various spacecraft systems. The application process is highly competitive, with only a small number of candidates selected every few years.

Those selected will train at NASA’s Johnson Space Center in Houston, Texas. Astronauts are expected to serve in various capacities, including mission control, technical assignments, and, ultimately, spaceflight missions.
